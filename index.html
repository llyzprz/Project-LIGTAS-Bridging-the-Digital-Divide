Understanding the Digital Divide

Our Advocacy

Project LIGTAS advocates for bridging the digital divide in Philippine public schools by ensuring fair and inclusive access to technology for all learners. Many students‚Äîespecially in rural areas‚Äîstill struggle with poor connectivity and limited digital tools. This advocacy aims to raise awareness on digital inequalities, strengthen collaboration among government, schools, and private sectors, promote digital literacy, and push for long-term policies that make technology a bridge rather than a barrier to education.

The Current Reality

Technology has become essential for learning, yet many public schools continue to face limited internet connectivity, outdated software, and insufficient digital literacy training. Only about 48% of public schools have internet access, and less than 2% offer free Wi-Fi, leaving many learners disadvantaged (Department of Education, 2022; European Union Global Campus of Human Rights, 2023).

This gap‚Äîknown as the digital divide‚Äîis fueled by unequal access to devices and stable internet, which has intensified learning inequalities (Sarmiento & Orale, 2021; UNICEF & World Bank, 2022).

Impact of the Pandemic

The COVID-19 pandemic further widened this gap, as millions of students struggled with remote learning due to lack of digital support (Albert et al., 2023). UNICEF and the World Bank (2022) reported that:

91% of Filipino 10-year-olds cannot read and understand simple text

This reflects inequities tied to poverty and technological access. Students in remote communities continue to face the largest disadvantages (De La Fuente, 2022; Macrothink Institute, 2023).

Moving Forward

While efforts‚Äîsuch as the government's plan to provide internet to all public schools‚Äîare underway (Philstar, 2025), significant gaps remain in infrastructure, implementation, and teacher readiness (World Bank, 2022). Addressing the divide goes beyond distributing gadgets; it requires strengthening digital learning systems and ensuring equitable and effective access for all learners (PIDS, 2023).

TAKE ACTION

Proposed Actions

Five strategic initiatives to bridge the digital divide

A. Campus Gadget Donation Drive

Collect used laptops, tablets, and smartphones from individuals, schools, and corporations. Set up donation booths on campus where students and faculty can drop off their old devices. Partner with tech repair shops or student volunteers to clean, refurbish, and test donated gadgets. Distribute devices to students identified by teachers and barangay officials as lacking digital access.

B. Printed Digital Literacy Starter Kits

Create simple, illustrated guides in both Tagalog and English covering basic digital skills. Design the kits to be easy to understand for students with limited tech experience. Distribute these printed materials alongside donated gadgets to ensure recipients can maximize their use of technology.

C. "Adopt-One-School" Pilot Partnership

Select one public school in a rural or underserved area as a pilot partner. Deliver refurbished gadgets and digital literacy kits to identified students. Conduct orientation sessions with teachers and students on how to use the devices and materials effectively. Gather feedback from the school community to refine the program before scaling to additional schools.

D. Low-Cost Connectivity Options

Promote affordable internet access solutions such as Piso Wi-Fi, community hubs, and DICT hotspots. Create visual maps and infographics showing where students can access free or low-cost internet in their communities. Coordinate with local government units to activate and maintain these connectivity points. Encourage schools to guide students on how to locate and use these resources for their educational needs.

E. Awareness Micro-Campaign

Launch a social media campaign using short videos and infographics. to raise awareness about the digital divide. Create shareable infographics highlighting key statistics and the impact of digital inequality. Post daily content during the donation week to encourage participation and engagement. Use hashtag #ProjectLIGTAS and post daily during donation weeks.
